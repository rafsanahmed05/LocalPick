# LocalPick

## 1. Introduction

### 1.1 Overview
LocalPick is a community-centered, location-aware marketplace that connects customers to nearby shops. It delivers real-time inventory, smart price negotiation, and tracked delivery for groceries, daily essentials, and household items. A built-in negotiation system lets customers bargain with shop owners online—mirroring traditional markets—while supporting affordability, personalization, and stronger customer–retailer relationships.

### 1.2 Motivation
- Speed and convenience: modern lifestyles demand rapid access to necessities.
- Empowering local retailers: a robust online channel lets small shops compete digitally.
- Inclusive growth: flexible part-time delivery work supplements community income.
- Smarter commerce through AI: better discovery, order management, and delivery optimization.
- Strong last-mile execution: reduced delays and higher customer satisfaction.

### 1.3 Problem Definition
#### 1.3.1 Problem Statement
- Slow access to essentials due to limited fast-delivery options.
- No real-time negotiation on most platforms; AI-driven bargaining boosts engagement and conversion.
- Fragmented delivery operations causing operational bottlenecks.
- Limited flexible earning options for part-time delivery personnel.
- High barriers for small shops to digitize and manage online sales.
- Generic experiences lacking personalization and advanced AI features.

### 1.4 Goals / Objectives
- Launch an efficient platform for local shops to digitize inventory and manage orders.
- Provide an intuitive UI for browsing, ordering, and real-time delivery tracking.
- Use AI to streamline order processing and delivery efficiency.
- Enable structured buyer–seller negotiation within defined limits.

### 1.5 Application
- Online shopping: browse, search, and buy from nearby shops.
- Small business enablement: digitize stores, manage stock, and reach more customers.
- Order management: track and update orders for customers and sellers.
- Delivery coordination: assign deliveries, track status, and confirm in real time.
- Admin oversight: monitor the system, manage users, and analyze performance.

### 1.6 Functional Requirements
#### 1.6.1 Customer Module
- User registration and authentication
- Product browsing
- Shopping cart
- Order placement
- Order tracking
- Price negotiation

#### 1.6.2 Delivery Driver Module
- Driver registration
- Order acceptance
- Delivery status updates
- Earnings and payouts

#### 1.6.3 Admin Module
- User management
- Order monitoring
- Reports and analytics

#### 1.6.4 AI Module
- Product recommendations
- Inventory optimization
- Route optimization
- Dynamic pricing insight

### 1.7 Non-Functional Requirements
- Reliability
- Usability
- Security
- Scalability

### 1.8 Technologies and Frameworks
#### 1.8.1 Frontend Technologies
HTML, CSS, Tailwind CSS, and JavaScript deliver a fast, responsive, and user-friendly interface for customers, shop owners, and delivery drivers.

#### 1.8.2 Backend Technologies
Node.js and Express.js provide scalable APIs for authentication, product management, orders, negotiation, and real-time delivery operations.

#### 1.8.3 Database Management
MongoDB stores users, shops, products, inventory, orders, and negotiation records with flexible, high-performance access.

#### 1.8.4 Payment Systems
Secure options include Cash on Delivery (COD) and mobile banking (bKash, Nagad, Rocket) for safe, convenient transactions.
